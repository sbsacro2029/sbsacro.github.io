The name "Intramuros" translates to "within the walls" because the city is surrounded by tall stone walls that are used to defend against threats.

What lies within the walled city is Fort Santiago. Built in 1571, the site is known for its prisons.



The San Agustin Church is the only one of the seven original churches of Intramuros that survived the heavy bombings during the battle of Manila in 1945.

Nowadays, the church is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1993.
